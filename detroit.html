The Motor City, Detroit

Detroit is home to rough 640,000 citizens as of 2020(“Explore Census Data.”, n.d.). Detroit became the United States in 1796 but did not become a city until 1815(“Detroit History.”, n.d.). Detroit is located in the south-eastern part of the Lower Peninsula. Detroit's median household income is nearly half of Michigan's state median, sitting at only $38,080(“Explore Census Data.”, n.d.). Detroit is under urban classification for the city ("Appendix B: Rural and Urban County Classification.", n.d.).

Detroit Landmarks

Monument to Joe Louis

Joe Louis was a heavyweight boxer who moved to Detroit before becoming a world championship. He fought Nazis on the battlefield and in the ring, which made him a national hero (“Explore Detroit’s Most Iconic Landmarks | Visit Detroit | Visit Detroit.”, 2024).

Ambassador Bridge

Nearly 100 years old, the Ambassador Bridge has connected Detroit to Windsor, Ontario. The suspension bridge was a architectural feat, being the world's largest suspension bridge for two years (“Explore Detroit’s Most Iconic Landmarks | Visit Detroit | Visit Detroit.”, 2024).

The Spirit of Detroit

A statue of a human holding a sphere that resembles the sun and a family. As a figure of Detroit, The Spirit of Detroit is adorned with jerseys during playoff season (“Explore Detroit’s Most Iconic Landmarks | Visit Detroit | Visit Detroit.”, 2024).

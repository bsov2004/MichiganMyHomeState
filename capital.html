The Capital of Michigan, Lansing

Lansing has a population of around 110,000 people as of 2023 (“Explore Census Data.”, n.d.). Lansing became a city of Michigan in 1859 (“City Facts | Lansing, MI - Official Website.”, n.d.). Located in the south-central part of the Lower Peninsula, Lansing is surrounded by several other popular cities. Lansing is classified as an urban area with a city, suburbs, and a metropolitan area. The average income of citizens of Lansing rank lower than the state average, averaging only $52,170 per household compared to the state median of $63,202 (“Explore Census Data.”, n.d.).

Lansing Landmarks

Spartan Stadium

An outdoor stadium in East Lansing that opened in 1923. Primarily used for football, Spartan Stadium is home to Michigan State University Spartans (“Spartan Stadium - Michigan State University Athletics.”, 2023).

Potter Park Zoo

A 102-acre zoo inspired to conserve animals in the natural world. Potter Park Zoo is the oldest zoo in Michigan and home to over 160 species of animals (“Potter Park Zoo.”, n.d.).

Michigan History Center

A state agency that preserves and shares the stories of Michigan. Experience interactive exhibits and educational programs (“Michigan History Center.”, n.d.).
